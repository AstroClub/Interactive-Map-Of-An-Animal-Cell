Golgi Apparatus

Functions:

The golgi apparatus functions like a factory, where proteins are prepared for transport to other areas of the cell.

• These proteins enter through the "cis" face, closest to the endoplasmic reticulum (ER), in clusters of fused transport vesicles. Vesicles are small sacs formed by the membrane wrapping around substances in order to transport them across the cell.

• This cluster eventually fuses with the cis membrane, and the contents are delivered to the lumen. The lumen is responsible for protein modification, using enzymes to change the portion of carbohydrates/sugars within the protein by adding or subtracting individual sugar monomers, amongst other modifications.

• These modified proteins are finally packaged into vesicles at the "trans" face, where they are either transported to other areas of the cell or secreted outside the cell through exocytosis.
